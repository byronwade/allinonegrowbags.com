<GuideStep number={1} title="Preparation">
    Before beginning your mushroom growing journey, ensure you have all necessary supplies:
    
    - Clean workspace or still air box
    - Disposable gloves
    - 70% isopropyl alcohol
    - Paper towels
    - ZugzBag all-in-one grow bag
    - Spore syringe or liquid culture
    - Temperature control method
</GuideStep>

<GuideStep number={2} title="Workspace Setup">
    Create a clean environment for your grow:

    - Choose a room with minimal air movement
    - Clean all surfaces with isopropyl alcohol
    - Set up your temperature control system
    - Ensure proper lighting is available
    
    <ResponsiveImage 
        src="/3_how-to-use-all-in-one-grow-bags.jpg" 
        alt="Clean Workspace Setup" 
        caption="Example of a properly prepared workspace"
    />
</GuideStep>

<GuideStep number={3} title="Sanitization">
    Proper sanitization is crucial for success:
    
    - Put on fresh gloves
    - Clean the injection port thoroughly
    - Wipe down all tools with alcohol
    - Allow surfaces to dry completely
    
    <Callout type="warning" icon="⚠️">
        Skip this step, and you risk contamination that could ruin your entire grow.
    </Callout>
</GuideStep>

<GuideStep number={4} title="Inoculation">
    Time to introduce your spores or culture:
    
    - Shake your spore syringe well
    - Clean the needle with alcohol
    - Insert through the injection port
    - Inject 2-3cc of solution
    
    <ResponsiveImage 
        src="/3_how-to-use-all-in-one-grow-bags.jpg" 
        alt="Proper Inoculation Technique" 
        caption="Demonstrating correct injection port usage"
    />
</GuideStep>

<GuideStep number={5} title="Colonization">
    Create optimal conditions for growth:
    
    - Maintain temperature at 70-75°F
    - Keep away from direct sunlight
    - Store upright in a clean area
    - Check daily for signs of growth
</GuideStep>

## Next Steps

Once you've completed these initial steps, you'll need to monitor your grow bag for colonization. This process typically takes 2-3 weeks.
